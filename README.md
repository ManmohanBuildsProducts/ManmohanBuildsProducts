### § 01 · The shift

Seven years as a product leader — Blinkit (#11 on the offline vertical, pre-Zomato), Tago Care, Bijak, and now Head of Product at a B2B commerce platform where I also stood up the internal AI/automation function from scratch.

Somewhere in the middle of that, I stopped being a PM who writes PRDs and became a PM who **builds**.

The arc, briefly:

**n8n** taught me that most "product work" is actually wiring. Triggers, pipelines, enrichers, evaluators. Ship a workflow, watch a metric move, come back the next morning.

**Cursor** taught me that a code editor is really a thinking partner with a diff view. Tab-complete became draft-a-module.

**Claude Code** is where it got serious. Agents, MCP servers, sub-agents, skills, hooks, long-horizon plans that survive a 200-turn session. I don't "use AI to write code" — I design systems where Claude is a first-class engineer on the team, and I'm the one holding the spec.

That's what I want to do full-time. This profile is the work in public while I get there.

### § 03 · What I'm thinking about

- **Agents aren't chatbots — they're teammates with permissions.** The interesting design surface is the permission model, the memory, the hooks, the plan/review/ship loop. The prompt is the least interesting part.
- **Vertical AI beats horizontal AI wherever the vocabulary is weird.** Auto parts, Indian retail, regulatory text — anywhere a generic embedding model loses 30+ points on your queries, there's a product.
- **Write ADRs or regret it.** A solo builder without decision records is a solo builder shipping the same mistake twice.
- **Claude Code + a personal memory system + a skills library is, right now, the highest-leverage setup I've found for one person shipping real software.** The one-person-company meme is real because the tooling finally caught up.
